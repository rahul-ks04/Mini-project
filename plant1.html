Common Plant Diseases

Learn about frequently occurring plant diseases, their symptoms, and how to effectively treat them.

Pepper Bell Bacterial Spot

Symptoms

  • Scabby, irregular lesions on leaves and fruit
  • Leaf drop
  • Reduced yield

Treatment

Apply copper-based bactericides every 7-10 days. Avoid overhead watering to minimize moisture and practice crop rotation to prevent further infection.

Potato Early Blight

Symptoms

  • Brown lesions with concentric rings on leaves and stems
  • Yellowing and defoliation
  • Dark, sunken lesions on tubers

Treatment

Use fungicides like chlorothalonil or mancozeb. Ensure proper plant spacing for air circulation, and practice crop rotation with non-host plants like corn or beans.

Potato Late Blight

Symptoms

  • Water-soaked lesions on leaves and stems
  • White mold growth under leaves
  • Brown, firm rot in tubers

Treatment

Apply fungicides containing chlorothalonil or copper hydroxide. Avoid overhead watering and use resistant potato varieties like 'Defender' or 'Elba.'

Tomato Bacterial Spot

Symptoms

  • Small, water-soaked spots on leaves and fruit
  • Spots may merge, causing leaf yellowing and drop
  • Scabby lesions on fruit

Treatment

Apply copper-based bactericides regularly. Avoid working with wet plants, use disease-free seeds, and rotate crops to non-host plants like legumes.

Tomato Early Blight

Symptoms

  • Dark, concentric lesions on older leaves
  • Yellowing and defoliation
  • Lesions on stems and fruit near the stem end

Treatment

Use fungicides such as chlorothalonil, mancozeb, or copper-based products. Mulch around the plant base to reduce soil splash and maintain proper spacing for airflow.

Tomato Late Blight

Symptoms

  • Large, water-soaked lesions on leaves and stems
  • White mold growth under leaves
  • Brown, firm rot in fruit

Treatment

Apply fungicides like mancozeb or fluazinam. Provide adequate ventilation and spacing between plants, and use disease-resistant varieties like 'Mountain Magic.'

Tomato Leaf Mold

Symptoms

  • Yellow spots on upper leaf surfaces
  • Olive-green to gray mold on undersides
  • Leaf drop

Treatment

Apply fungicides like chlorothalonil or copper-based products. Improve air circulation by ensuring wider plant spacing and avoid overhead watering.

Tomato Septoria Leaf Spot

Symptoms

  • Small, circular spots with gray centers and dark borders on leaves
  • Yellowing and defoliation
  • Reduced yield

Treatment

Apply fungicides containing chlorothalonil or mancozeb. Ensure good air circulation, avoid overhead watering, and practice crop rotation.

Tomato Spider Mites

Symptoms

  • Speckled or bronzed leaves
  • Fine webbing on leaves and stems
  • Stunted growth

Treatment

Use insecticidal soap or neem oil spray. Introduce natural predators like ladybugs, maintain humidity levels, and rinse plants with water to dislodge mites.

Tomato Target Spot

Symptoms

  • Brown spots with concentric rings on leaves
  • Lesions on stems and fruit
  • Leaf drop

Treatment

Apply fungicides like azoxystrobin or chlorothalonil. Ensure proper spacing for air circulation, avoid overhead watering, and keep the plant environment dry.

Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus

Symptoms

  • Yellowing and upward curling of leaves
  • Stunted growth
  • Reduced fruit set

Treatment

Control whitefly populations using insecticides or reflective mulch. Use virus-resistant tomato varieties like 'TYLCV-resistant' hybrids.

Tomato Mosaic Virus

Symptoms

  • Mottled light and dark green patterns on leaves
  • Leaf curling and distortion
  • Stunted growth

Treatment

Disinfect tools and hands after plant contact, and apply insecticidal soap to control aphids and other virus-transmitting pests. Use disease-resistant seeds to prevent future infections.
